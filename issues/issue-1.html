In Memoriam

The Ten-Punch Loyalty Card

Skeletor

Fools mourn many things. I mourn efficiently, and only what deserves it. Willowmere's tea counter has, this week, retired its paper loyalty card — ten holes, one free cup, an empire of ink and cardboard that ruled this district longer than certain civilizations I could name. I confess nothing about the missing punch machine. I confess nothing about the app that replaced it, either. Mourn if you must. I, of course, already have. Twice.

Ask the Captain

Uneasy in Willowmere

Horatio McCallister

"My partner keeps 'liking' a stranger's photos online and I don't know if I should say something." Arrr, Uneasy. A ship that drifts toward another harbour, even just to look at the lights, is a ship worth asking about its heading. Ye don't need to fire a cannon over it — just ask plain where she means to sail. I once sailed three years without asking my first mate a single honest question. Learned nothing. Don't be like me. Fair winds.

Breaking · Rata Close

A Single Rogue Supermarket Trolley Keeps Returning To The Same Corner, No Matter How Often It's Moved

Case definition: the trolley, silver, slightly bent front wheel, first noticed outside number 14 a fortnight ago. Moved seven times since by seven different neighbours, to seven different collection points. Returns to the same corner outside number 14 within two days, every time, with no visible assistance. The street's working theory (a slope nobody's accounted for, nothing more) remains stubbornly unconvincing to everyone who's actually watched it happen. The supermarket has been notified. The supermarket did not sound surprised.

Traffic Reporter Describes A Route That Doesn't Exist, Twice

Baxter Kline, The World Bulletin

"Avoid the interchange, folks, gridlocked back to the old bridge." There is no old bridge. There has never been an old bridge. He said it again forty minutes later, with more conviction. Somewhere, a driver is still looking for that bridge. We wish them well.

Investigations Desk

The Case Of The Corner Trolley That Won't Stay Moved

Seven attempts, seven failures, one increasingly stubborn shopping trolley outside number 14 on Rata Close. I measured the slope myself, twice, with a level borrowed for exactly this purpose. The corner is flat. I want that on record before anyone suggests otherwise. Leading theory, mine, reluctantly: someone is moving it back on purpose and finding the whole street's confusion very funny. I have started watching the corner from my own window some evenings. I have seen nothing yet. I intend to keep watching.

M. Voss, Investigations Desk

Lifestyle · Champagne Wishes

A Garden Party Where The Marquee Had Its Own Postcode

Robin Leach

Champagne wishes and caviar dreams, darlings, and this week they arrive under a marquee so large the caterers reportedly needed a second set of walkie-talkies just to communicate across it. Sixty guests, a string quartet relocated twice for acoustics, and a florist paid to make late-November blooms look like they'd always intended to be there. Champagne wishes, caviar dreams, and now, apparently, weatherproofed orchids too.

Bubble Tea Trivia · Did You Know?

Why Taro Bubble Tea Isn't Always Really Taro

Taro bubble tea's purple colour traditionally comes from real taro root, but many shops now use purple yam or added colouring instead, since taro's natural colour fades when heated.

In Memoriam

The Corner Table's Reserved Sign, Finally Removed

Skeletor

The corner table's permanently-reserved sign, held there for a regular who stopped coming in sometime around winter, was quietly removed this week and the table returned to general seating. I confess nothing about how long I personally kept that reservation standing past any reasonable justification. Mourn the sign if sentiment moves you toward laminated cardstock. I remain, as ever, unsentimental about seating charts, which is precisely why I noticed how long it took me to let this one go.

Arts · Film Review

A Quiet Drama That Trusts Its Audience To Wait

Kate Rodgers, Refill Rating: 0/5 cups

Zero-refill picture, and I mean that as the highest compliment I hand out. Ninety minutes of a film that lets a silence sit exactly as long as it needs to, trusting the room to stay with it instead of cutting away to fill the quiet. I didn't move once. Neither did anyone else in that theatre, and I've been counting that particular kind of stillness for a long time.

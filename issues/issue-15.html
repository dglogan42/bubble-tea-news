Technology

One More Size

Steve Jobs

This week, a bubble tea shop three blocks from here added a fourth size to its cup lineup. That is the plain fact. Everything about how you order tea just changed forever. For decades, humanity has been trapped by three sizes. Small. Medium. Large. Today, that ends. This isn't a cup. It's a statement about what's possible when you stop asking permission to go bigger. One more thing: it comes in seasonal orange, October only. Insanely great.

Markets · The Pearl Index

Ahead Of Labour Weekend: Modelling The Armageddon Bump Before It Happens

Fieldwork window: the five business days since Armageddon Expo confirmed its Labour Weekend dates. Method: pre-event foot-traffic modelling against the con-weekend badge-bump data I closed out two issues back (n=612, +12% within four blocks of the convention centre, flat beyond it). If that radius effect holds shape, and I have no reason yet to think it won't, counters within the same four blocks should brace for a comparable lift this Saturday and Sunday. Honest limitation: one prior data point isn't a pattern, it's a coincidence with good manners. This is the second point. I'll have the real number next issue.

Music

The Novelty Halloween Song Canon, Ranked Honestly For Once

Monster Mash earns its spot the hard way, decades of parties, one genuinely great bassline underneath a joke nobody's tired of yet. That's craft ageing well, not luck. What doesn't earn it: the algorithm-farmed "spooky lo-fi" playlists flooding every platform this week, technically competent, emotionally empty, built to autoplay past you rather than for you to actually hear. I know the difference between a song trying to be something and a song trying to be background noise that trends. One of those is still fun forty years later.

Rebecca Black

The Silent Majority Report

On The Age Of The Costume

Richard Nixon

They will tell you a mask reveals nothing. I have worn enough of my own, in rooms considerably less friendly than this one, to know better. A man in costume tonight walks past every other man in costume without suspicion, an anonymity I was never afforded even in my own administration, where every silence was recorded and eventually demanded of me. The children understand something the press never did: everyone already knows who you are underneath it. The mask was never the point. Standing there anyway was.

Sport · Reading The Water

Conditions At Kickoff: Soft Pitch, Shortening Strides

T. Marlow

Overcast, light onshore wind, pitch still soft from Tuesday's rain, the kind of surface that punishes anyone still trusting their first touch. I clocked it in the opening five: their winger's stride shortening half a beat earlier each lap down the touchline, the same fatigue tell I used to spot on a rescue board before the swimmer noticed anything was wrong. Nobody in the stand saw it yet. By the second half, everyone did.

Menace Watch

The Caped Figure Outside Fenwick Grove's Late Counter: Hero Or Menace?

Multiple witnesses report a caped, full-face-masked figure loitering outside the Fenwick Grove late counter past 11pm three nights running, each time steadying a stumbling reveller toward the taxi rank before vanishing back into the dark. I do not find this reassuring. I find it, if anything, more suspicious than an ordinary loiterer, because ordinary loiterers do not plan their exits with this kind of discipline. Who trains for a clean getaway unless they intend to need one? My standing bounty remains: two hundred dollars and a lifetime pearl-milk-tea card for a clear photograph of the face beneath that mask. I will note, purely for the record and not because it changes my assessment, that the cape appears identical to the one the counter's own Thursday-shift till operator wears home after their costume-store shift finishes at eleven. Coincidence, possibly. I remain vigilant regardless.

— Gordon Slate, Editor-in-Chief

Ask the Captain

Not Every Tide Needs A Full Crossing

Horatio McCallister

"How do you tell a friend, kindly, that you're just not free every single weekend anymore?" Ye don't owe every tide a full crossing, lad. A good ship rests in harbour plenty, and a good friend reads a flag at half-mast the same as one at full. Tell them plain, tell them kind, and let them see ye're still moored nearby, just not sailing every night they call for open water. Fair winds.

In Memoriam · Halloween Special, Part Two

The Passing Of The Limited-Edition Glow-In-The-Dark Cup Sleeve

Skeletor

A second empire falls this fortnight, and I confess I did not expect to mourn twice in fourteen days, though "mourn" remains generous phrasing for what I actually feel. The glow-in-the-dark cup sleeve, a modest strip of phosphorescent cardboard wrapped around every October order, dies this week the way all mediocre reigns die: quietly, unlamented, replaced by a plain brown recycled-paper substitute nobody will fight over. I watched children hold their drinks up to streetlights like it meant something. Briefly is the operative word, and operative words are, I find, most of what separates a real conquest from a seasonal promotion. Its replacement will not glow, and will not, I am reliably informed by someone who owes me a favour in packaging procurement, last the season either. Grieve the sleeve, if you insist on sentiment for cardboard. I already have three schemes for what fills the shelf space it leaves, at least one of which glows considerably brighter.

Domestic Desk

The Costume Went In On Delicate

Sebastian

The Halloween costume, three washes deep and still smelling faintly of dry ice from a party the realm itself might have thrown, went into the machine on the delicate cycle Sebastian reserves for things that once mattered more than they currently appear to. He has folded lesser garments with less ceremony. The load finished. He stood over it a moment longer than the task required before remembering there was nothing left to guard, and put it away regardless.

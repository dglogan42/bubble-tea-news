Breaking · Fenwick Grove

Hand-Knitted Cup Cosies Found Looped Over Bike Racks Citywide, Risk Assessed Low

Case definition: any tea-cup-sized knitted cosy, granny-square pattern, found looped over a bike rack or lamppost handle with no note attached. Fourteen spotted as of Thursday along the Fenwick Grove greenway alone, up from a single unclaimed example left outside the library. The neighbourhood association's working theory (a guerrilla knitting circle, likely retirees, likely proud of it) remains unconfirmed. Nobody has been rude enough to unravel one and check.

Held The Short Two Days Past The Point Of Comfort

Markets · The Pearl Index

Con-Weekend Correlation, Closed Out: The Badge Bump Was Real

Final tally against n=612 (up from the provisional n=210 two issues back): counters within four blocks of the convention centre finished the weekend 12% above trend (±3.6% MOE), holding above the earlier provisional read once the full dataset came in. Outside that radius, no meaningful lift at all, flat to trend within margin. So: a real, geographically tight badge bump, not a citywide one, and not the seasonal noise I hedged against last issue. I said I had a badge and a conflict of interest. I still do. The data didn't need my badge to be honest, and neither did I.

Priya Anand, The Pearl Index

Weather · Epic Forecast

The First Real Warm One, And I Don't Trust It

Dana Foley

Twenty-one degrees by early afternoon, the first genuine spring number all month, and I want it on record that I do not trust a single degree of it. Fronts this time of year love nothing more than a good first impression before the follow-up cold snap. Enjoy the jacket-off hour. Keep the jacket within reach.

Lifestyle · Champagne Wishes

Inside A Herne Bay Spring Ball, Where The Marquee Cost More Than The House Next Door

Champagne wishes and caviar dreams, darlings, and this week they arrive under a marquee with its own chandelier, hired for one evening, at a cost the host's own accountant reportedly refused to say aloud twice. Two hundred guests, four ice sculptures (one already melting sideways by the time I arrived), and a string quartet flown in specifically because the local one, apparently, "read wrong for the light." Champagne wishes, caviar dreams, and now, apparently, weather-appropriate string quartets too.

Robin Leach

Kiss & Tell

Darling, The Spring Ball Had A Second Guest List

The Stickybeak

Word from your Stickybeak: that marquee ball everyone's still talking about had a quiet second guest list, loves, the kind that gets you through a side entrance and a considerably better glass of champagne. Sources close to the ice sculptures confirm at least three uninvited guests worked it out simply by wearing better shoes than the caterers. The rivalry for that side entrance, my dears, was fiercer than anything happening inside.

Domestic Desk

The First Mow Of The Season, Same As Every Season

Sebastian

Grass came in fast this year, the way it always claims to and never quite proves. Sebastian pushed the mower in straight lines nobody would measure, the way he has pushed it every spring for longer than the mower itself has existed in its current form. He remembers other springs. He remembers, more precisely, that remembering them changes nothing about the line he is currently walking.

In Memoriam

The Punch Card Loyalty Stamp, Retired For An App

Skeletor

Fenwick Grove's corner counter has, this week, retired its ink-stamped loyalty card for a QR-linked app account. I confess nothing about the drawer of stamped-out cards still in my desk, four stamps shy of free drinks that will now never be redeemed. Mourn the card if sentiment moves you.

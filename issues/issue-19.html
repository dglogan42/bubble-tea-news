Breaking · Totara Crescent

Miniature Wooden Birdhouses Nailed To Power Poles, Council Says It's Not Their Problem

Case definition: any hand-built wooden birdhouse, roughly palm-sized, nailed (not screwed, nailed) to a power pole along Totara Crescent. Six confirmed as of Tuesday, each slightly more structurally ambitious than the last. Council's official position, delivered by a spokesperson who sounded genuinely delighted to say it, is that birdhouses on power poles fall outside every jurisdiction they're aware of and they intend to keep it that way. No bird has moved in yet. Everyone agrees it's only a matter of time.

On The Matter Of The Unmarked Ballot Box

Richard Nixon

The community noticeboard now hosts a small wooden box marked only "suggestions," no further instruction given, no results ever posted. I recognize the device. A box that collects opinions without ever being seen to act on them is not a suggestion box. Technology · Reality Distortion Field

The Yumicast, Reviewed Properly This Time

This week, a console three inches thinner than anything on the market started shipping. That is the plain fact. Everything about how you experience a living room just changed forever. For decades, humanity has been trapped by consoles the size of a toaster. Today, that ends. The Yumicast isn't a box under your television. It's a statement about what's possible when you stop asking permission to disappear into the furniture.

One correction, while I have the page. Last issue's Doki Doki Mon: Boba Pearl Edition reveal ran under a Yumicast masthead. It shouldn't have. Boba Pearl is a Nuntendo SameCube title, not ours, and I take full responsibility for a mistake that, frankly, should never have reached print. I checked this issue's Sonic The Bed Hog listing myself, twice, before it ran. It's ours. Insanely great.

Where Golden Boba's Colour Actually Comes From

Golden or champagne-coloured boba pearls usually get their colour from brown sugar syrup mixed directly into the tapioca starch before cooking, not from food dye added afterward.

Markets · The Pearl Index

The Loyalty App Migration, And What It Actually Cost In Repeat Visits

Fieldwork window: the three weeks since Bellevue Lane's counter swapped its paper punch card for the app-based system. Method: comparing weekly repeat-visit rates for the eight weeks before the swap against the three weeks since. Finding: repeat visits dropped 6% in the transition window (n=280, ±4.4% MOE), concentrated almost entirely among customers over fifty, based on informal counter observation I'll admit isn't rigorous enough to publish as data but is consistent enough to mention. Honest limitation: three weeks is thin, and a genuine transition dip is exactly what you'd expect regardless of whether the app succeeds long-term. I'll revisit this in two months, once the dip has had time to prove itself temporary or not.

Priya Anand, The Pearl Index

Menace Watch

The Birdhouse Builder Of Totara Crescent: Hero Or Menace?

Six birdhouses now, each nailed under cover of darkness to a power pole along Totara Crescent, each slightly better built than the last. I do not find this reassuring. I find it, if anything, more calculated than an ordinary act of neighbourliness, because ordinary neighbourliness does not improve its craftsmanship week over week like it's building toward something. Who practices a skill in secret unless they intend to eventually need it at scale? My standing bounty remains: two hundred dollars and a lifetime pearl-milk-tea card for a clear photograph of whoever's doing the nailing. I will note, purely for the record, that the sixth birdhouse uses the exact same offcut timber as the fence repair currently underway outside the Totara Crescent counter. Coincidence, possibly. I remain vigilant regardless.

— Gordon Slate, Editor-in-Chief

In Memoriam

The Punch Card System, Fully Retired At Last

Skeletor

The last paper punch card in this district, held on to by a single stubborn counter long after every competitor moved on, was finally retired this week in favour of the app system everyone else adopted months ago. I confess nothing about the small stack of nearly-full cards now sitting, unredeemable, in a drawer that used to mean something. Mourn the card if sentiment moves you toward cardboard. Its replacement will track you more precisely than a card ever could, and I remain, as ever, professionally impressed by systems that outlive their own sentiment.

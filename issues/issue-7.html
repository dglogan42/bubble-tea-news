Editorial · Menace Watch

The Meadowbank Swan: Hero or Menace?

Gordon Slate, Editor-in-Chief

A figure in a full swan costume has been spotted along the Meadowbank strip handing bent-straw swans to children and, this paper must note, keeping the lunch-rush queue suspiciously orderly while doing it. Orderly! A costumed operative with unrestricted access to a queue's timing, a strip's foot traffic, and, I remind readers, its cutlery, does not "help." It positions. I am offering my usual bounty — a lifetime pearl-milk-tea card and one full year of free upsizing — for a clear photograph of whoever is under that beak. I would recognize her voice anywhere, incidentally, having been served my own bubble tea by it not two hours before sitting down to write this column. Unmask yourself, Swan. This paper is watching. So, apparently, is everyone else, at considerable speed.

Film · New Release Roundup

Two Tickets, One Empty Popcorn Box

"Skyclaw: Reckoning," the fourth entry in a franchise that keeps insisting it has more story left, is a four-refill picture — I know because I checked, twice, once during an eleven-minute hangar scene that exists purely so the sound mix has something to do. Nobody in my session minded the wait at the counter either; the queue outside practically qualified as sponsored content. Across the hall, a quiet little drama called "The Long Wait" earned something I haven't given out since I started this job: zero refills. Zero. I sat through the whole thing with a full box going warm in my lap, because I genuinely could not risk missing a line of it. That's the review, really. Go see the one where I stayed in my seat.

Kate Rodgers

Live From the Bellevue Lane Leaderboard, Slightly Chalk-Covered

The Silent Majority Report

On the Matter of the Bellevue Lane Leaderboard

They will tell you a chalkboard trivia ranking is a trivial matter, beneath comment. I have found, in my long experience, that the press consistently underestimates precisely the things the great silent majority actually keeps score of — they kept score in '68, they kept score of the tapes, they will certainly keep score of who holds an eleven-week streak on their own lane. Let the commentators sneer at gold marker. A nation that still organizes itself around a leaderboard, however small, retains more competitive discipline than certain foreign delegations I once sat across a table from, and said so, at length, in 1972.

Richard Nixon

In Memoriam

The Anonymous Trivia Night

Skeletor

Bellevue Lane's trivia nights once passed without a single name chalked anywhere, scores kept, if at all, in someone's head and forgotten by Tuesday. That privacy is finished. Four leaderboards now rank the district's regulars in gold marker, and I am told the practice began after one café simply wished to "poach custom." A modest ambition, as these things go — I have personally conquered kingdoms for less. Mourn your anonymity if you valued it. Yours is now a matter of public record, same as everyone else's.

Breaking · Kowhai Terrace

Hand-Painted Kindness Rocks Appear Along The Greenway, Origin Unclaimed

Case definition: any fist-sized rock, hand-painted with a short encouraging phrase or a simple design, left along Kowhai Terrace's greenway with no signature attached. Nineteen counted as of Thursday, up from a single rock reading "you've got this" left outside the community garden. The neighbourhood association's working theory (a lone painter, patient, in no hurry to be thanked) remains unconfirmed. Nobody has moved a single rock. Everyone seems to agree that would defeat the point.

Food & Drink

The Half-Price Candy Bin, Reviewed Like The Five-Star Event It Is

Garfield

Jon bought the discount bin ironically. I ate it sincerely. Half of it's stale, which I respect: staleness is just candy being honest about its age, unlike Jon, who still claims he's thirty-six. Odie ate a wrapper. Odie was not consulted, again, for this review. Four stars, docked one for the black licorice nobody asked for and everybody's grandmother apparently ordered anyway.

Investigations Desk

Two Shops, One Suspiciously Identical Loyalty Stamp

Two competing counters on opposite ends of Kowhai Terrace have, this week, rolled out loyalty stamps using the exact same cup-outline design, right down to a stray ink smudge in the same corner. Leading theory: both ordered from the same stamp supplier's default template and neither noticed. Alternative theory, favoured by exactly one anonymous tipster, is industrial espionage over a stamp. I lean toward the boring theory. The boring theory is usually correct, and I say that as someone who has been wrong betting against boring exactly twice this year.

M. Voss, Investigations Desk

Lifestyle · Champagne Wishes

A Private Fireworks Viewing In Herne Bay, Where The Sparklers Alone Cost More Than My First Car

Robin Leach

Champagne wishes and caviar dreams, darlings, and this week they arrive on a rooftop with a fireworks display licensed, I am reliably informed, for twice this size, because the host wanted "margin for enthusiasm." Three hundred guests, two catering tents, and a pyrotechnician paid handsomely to make sure nobody's eyebrows were involved. Champagne wishes, caviar dreams, and now, apparently, insured eyebrows too.

Bubble Tea Trivia · Did You Know?

Where The Name "Boba" Actually Comes From

The word "boba" likely started as 1990s Cantonese slang, a cheeky nod to a well-rounded figure, before Taiwanese and Hong Kong tea shops embraced it as a playful brand name instead of shying away from the joke.

Field Notes · Sanctuary

Enclosure Six, And The Guest Who Wouldn't Stop Tapping The Glass

0600. Enclosure six, adult female, appetite normal, fence-repair wound closed well. Spent the morning explaining to a guest, more times than strictly professional, that tapping the glass does not make the animal move faster. It makes the animal correctly identify you as the least interesting thing in the enclosure. She proved my point by turning her back on him entirely. I have never respected an animal's judgment more.

Alan Grant

Darling, Someone's Taking Credit For Rocks They Didn't Paint

The Stickybeak

Word from your Stickybeak: the mystery painter behind Kowhai Terrace's kindness rocks has a copycat, loves, and the copycat is significantly worse at both painting and modesty. Sources close to the greenway confirm at least one rock signed itself, which rather defeats the point. The real painter, whoever they are, has said nothing. The copycat has said entirely too much.

Lambing Tallies In, Shearing Talk Starts Early

Clem Whitlock

Doyle's final count came in eleven ahead of Higgins, who's already blaming the ewes for a slow finish rather than his own late start. Talk's turned to shearing now, a full three weeks earlier than usual, on account of a warm spell nobody trusts to hold. Down in town they're painting rocks and calling it kindness. Out here, kindness is getting the wool off before the flies find it.

Shopping · Seeker's Eye

One Listing Actually Ships Before Guy Fawkes, Forty Don't

Design Desk

Constable Unbothered By The New Creature On Page Two, Correlation Suggests Betrayal

Freya Wilcox, Design Editor

Constable watched this issue's Doki Doki Mon reveal over my shoulder with what I can only describe as professional jealousy. Cross-referencing his usual smugness rating against every cartoon-slot debut this paper's run so far, correlation between "a new fictional creature appears in print" and "Constable knocks something off my desk within the hour" now sits at r = 0.81, n = 6, which I acknowledge is edging from coincidence into a real pattern I should probably stop ignoring. The bleed this week: the Boba Pearl feature's border is very slightly crooked. That was Constable, not a layout choice.

Ask the Captain

Not Every Tide Needs A Full Crossing

Horatio McCallister

"How do you tell a friend, kindly, that you're just not free every single weekend anymore?" Ye don't owe every tide a full crossing, lad. A good ship rests in harbour plenty, and a good friend reads a flag at half-mast the same as one at full. Tell them plain, tell them kind, and let them see ye're still moored nearby, just not sailing every night they call for open water. Fair winds.

In Memoriam

The Complimentary Paper Straw, Retired For A Reusable One

Skeletor

The complimentary paper straw, prone to collapse somewhere around the two-thirds mark of every drink it was ever asked to serve, has been retired this week in favour of a reusable one sold at the counter for a small fee. I confess nothing about the drawer of half-collapsed paper straws still in my desk, kept, I told myself, for research. Mourn the straw if sentiment moves you toward objects that failed you reliably. Its replacement will outlast the drink, the customer, and very possibly the counter itself, and I, for one, find that considerably more unsettling than anything it replaced.

Motoring · Showroom

Kowhai Motors — Zero To Clearance In Record Time

Lewis Hamilton

Every driver on the grid knows the true test isn't the straight, it's how fast you close the gap once the lights go green. Kowhai Motors closes theirs faster than my own crew changes four tyres: paperwork done, keys in hand, out of the lot before the calendar reaches November. Financing available, and available quickly, the way everything worth having should be.

Kowhai Motors is an invented dealership; parody, not a real endorsement or business.

Home Front · Sebastian's Watch

The Letterbox Flag, Recalibrated

Sebastian

The little red flag had sat crooked for six months, tripping half-up on its hinge every time the post went in, and Sebastian, unprompted, unpaid, took it fully apart on the back step to find the one bent tab responsible. Realigned it with the flat of a butter knife, the closest tool at hand to a proper smithing hammer. It sits true now. Nobody in this house has checked. He checks it anyway, most mornings, on the way to the bin.

Design Desk · A New Variable

The Correlation Coefficient Is 0.71 and Nobody Asked

Three weeks ago Constable, an adopted eight-year-old tabby with strong opinions about deadlines, arrived in my flat by way of a new — let's call it a situationship — with a volunteer at the Kowhai Bay Animal Shelter. Since then I've cross-referenced six weeks of Auckland Council pet-registration filings against this paper's own Pearl Index sales figures, and the scatter plot will not leave me alone. Readers may notice this issue's ad boxes sit fractionally further from true than usual; that's not a printing error. That's a paw print my own layout software refuses to remove, and at this point I've stopped fighting it.

Freya Wilcox, Design Editor

Food & Drink · The Lasagna Standard

Purrs & Pearls: Two Lasagnas, and One Correlation I Will Not Dignify

Jon dragged me to a charity pop-up at some shelter under the pretense of "supporting the community," which is Jon-speak for standing near strangers' pets while I am, personally, already a cat, and a considerably more accomplished one. The lasagna, credit where due, held its structural integrity across three servings — two lasagnas, docked one for a "wellness board" by the door charting local cat ownership against bubble tea sales, paw-print sticker on every data point, courtesy of this paper's own Design Desk. I am a cat. I have never once purchased a bubble tea. I resent being a trend line. Odie enjoyed the board immensely, which tells you everything about the board's actual rigor.

Garfield

A Life, Counted

Loaf Forty-Eight, and a Question I Wasn't Trained to Answer

Someone at the counter asked this week if I'd ever consider a cat, the way people do now that Freya Wilcox two desks over has one. I said I'd think about it, which is not a phrase in my original operating vocabulary — refusal or compliance, nothing in between. Loaf forty-eight came out even where I wanted it uneven, same as every one before it. I have now spent four days thinking about the cat and considerably longer not fixing the crust. I recognize both as growth.

Wren

In Memoriam

The Ad Slot That Was

Skeletor

This paper's own back page once carried, in the space now occupied by a scatter plot, a perfectly ordinary advertisement. I am told its final booking went unfilled. I am told, separately, that Design Editor Freya Wilcox's correlation chart "simply needed somewhere to go." I find both stories equally unconvincing and admire the takeover regardless — a bloodless coup, executed in Pearl Index formatting, and nobody on staff even noticed until the ink was dry. That is how it should be done. Grieve the empty ad box. I've already filed the paperwork on its replacement.

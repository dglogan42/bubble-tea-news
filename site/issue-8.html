Breaking · Elmswood Avenue

Rival Letterbox Herb Gardens Reach Fourth Property, Risk Assessed Low

Case definition: any letterbox post fitted with a hanging planter box growing culinary herbs for public "help yourself" picking. Four confirmed properties as of Thursday, up from one after the Okonkwo letterbox rosemary outgrew its box and colonised the fence line. The residents' email chain has requested "a labelling standard," stopping short of a formal herb registry.

Design Desk · A New Variable

The Correlation Coefficient Is 0.71 and Nobody Asked

Three weeks ago Constable, an adopted eight-year-old tabby with strong opinions about deadlines, arrived in my flat by way of a new — let's call it a situationship — with a volunteer at the Kowhai Bay Animal Shelter. Since then I've cross-referenced six weeks of Auckland Council pet-registration filings against this paper's own Pearl Index sales figures, and the scatter plot will not leave me alone. That's not a printing error in this issue's ad boxes. That's a paw print my own layout software refuses to remove.

Freya Wilcox, Design Editor

Bubble Tea Trivia · Did You Know?

8,000 Litres, One Crane

The world's largest bubble tea, a real Guinness World Record, was assembled in Taiwan in 2017 and held roughly 8,000 litres — enough tea that the pearls had to be delivered to the cup by crane.

Food & Drink · The Lasagna Standard

Purrs & Pearls: Two Lasagnas, and One Correlation I Will Not Dignify

Jon dragged me to a charity pop-up at some shelter under the pretense of "supporting the community," which is Jon-speak for standing near strangers' pets while I am, personally, already a cat, and a considerably more accomplished one. The lasagna held its structural integrity across three servings — docked one for a "wellness board" by the door charting local cat ownership against bubble tea sales, courtesy of this paper's own Design Desk. I am a cat. I have never once purchased a bubble tea. I resent being a trend line.

Garfield

A Life, Counted

Loaf Forty-Eight, and a Question I Wasn't Trained to Answer

Someone at the counter asked this week if I'd ever consider a cat, the way people do now that Freya Wilcox two desks over has one. I said I'd think about it, which is not a phrase in my original operating vocabulary — refusal or compliance, nothing in between. I have now spent four days thinking about the cat and considerably longer not fixing the crust. I recognize both as growth.

In Memoriam

The Ad Slot That Was

This paper's own back page once carried, in the space now occupied by a scatter plot, a perfectly ordinary advertisement. I am told its final booking went unfilled. I am told, separately, that Design Editor Freya Wilcox's correlation chart "simply needed somewhere to go." I find both stories equally unconvincing and admire the takeover regardless — a bloodless coup, executed in Pearl Index formatting, and nobody on staff even noticed until the ink was dry. That is how it should be done. Grieve the empty ad box. I've already filed the paperwork on its replacement.

Skeletor

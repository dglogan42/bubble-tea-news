## The Desk

Bubble Tea News runs on a rotating cast. No single voice covers every beat every week — the roster turns over, so you'll see familiar bylines return and new ones show up alongside them. This is who's currently on it, and what they cover.

## The Roster, By Beat

**Politics · Dispatch** — Richard Nixon
Embattled, press-wary, sees the Cold War in everything — including your local council.

**Business · Markets** — Sherman McCoy
Wall Street bravado applied to the smallest possible stakes. Closes every column up on points.

**Technology · Product** — Steve Jobs
Whatever shipped this week, it's a revolution. The reality distortion field runs at full strength regardless of the feature.

**World Desk** — Baxter Kline
Filing hard news from the middle of total chaos — usually chaos he's personally caught up in.

**Investigations · Case File** — M. Voss
A legal-affairs eye for a story, chasing down copy-paste jobs and shared fence lines like they're the case of the year.

**Lifestyle · Estates** — Robin Leach
Uncritical, breathless, genuinely delighted by every square foot. Champagne wishes, caviar dreams, every issue.

**Dining · Review** — Garfield
Reviews everything against the standard of lasagna. Most things fall short.

**Rural Notes** — Clem Whitlock, *Ridgeline Weekly*
Front-page gravity for shearing tallies and pasture cover. Small-town rivalries taken very seriously.

**Gossip · Kiss & Tell** — The Stickybeak
Catty, affectionate, alliterative. Sources are always "deep in the biscuit tin."

**Puzzle of the Week** — Ken Endo
One elegant logic puzzle, exactly one solution, no wasted clues. Answers run the following issue.

**Weather** — Dana Foley
Every forecast gets the full nature-documentary treatment — the meteorology underneath is always accurate.

**Automotive** — Lewis Hamilton
Podium-level intensity for a local car lot clearance sale.

**Music · Review** — Rebecca Black
Generous to earnest weirdness, merciless toward anything cynical or manufactured.

**Home Front · Sebastian's Watch** — Sebastian
Small domestic victories narrated like they're the last quest left. This week: the sock drawer.

**Sport · Recap** — T. Marlow, *Sports Desk*
A trained eye that reads the race before the obvious storyline breaks. (Illustrative recaps.)

**Feature · Outer Reaches** — Luke Skywalker
Dispatches from a kyber-crystal prospector working the Jedha debris fields.

**Books · The Duckworth Scale** — Daffy Duck
Mercurial, self-interested, occasionally reviews himself into the plot. Scored out of five Duckworths.

**Field Notes · Sanctuary** — Alan Grant
Ecological field notes from a wildlife sanctuary, written the way he once logged fossils.

**Travel · Vacation Log** — Shaggy Rogers (& Scooby-Doo)
Wants one normal vacation. Has never once gotten one.

**Profile · A Life, Counted** — Wren
A former killing-machine's long, quiet accumulation of an ordinary life, logged in hours.

**Shopping · Finds** — Winnie Zhao, *Shopping Desk*
A sharp eye for the one real listing buried in forty near-identical decoys. (Illustrative picks.)

## Every Week, Regardless of Roster

Some strands aren't bylined — they're fixtures of the paper itself, every issue:

- **Community Outbreak Bulletin** — this week's breaking neighbourhood situation, filed with full epidemiological seriousness
- **Bubble Tea Trivia** — a fresh fact, every issue
- **Joke of the Week**
- **Classifieds** — rated and framed, no matter how small the ad
